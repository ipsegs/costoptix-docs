## What is Cost Optix?

Cost Optix helps engineering teams, finance teams, and executives understand and control cloud spending across every provider in one place — without the complexity of enterprise tools or the limitations of single-provider dashboards.

## Core Capabilities

**Unified Multi-Cloud Cost Visibility**
See all your cloud spending — Azure, AWS, GCP, Heroku, Kubernetes, and more — aggregated into a single dashboard with per-service, per-account, and cross-provider breakdowns.

**Anomaly Detection**
Cost Optix uses rolling Z-score and adaptive statistical algorithms to detect abnormal spending patterns at the service level, not just account totals. Alerts fire before your bill surprises you.

**Budget Management**
Set budgets per account or provider, define warning (80%) and critical (95%) thresholds, and receive real-time alerts via Slack, Microsoft Teams, Discord, or any custom webhook.

**AI-Powered Insights**
Get natural-language cost optimization recommendations powered by AI, tailored to your actual spending patterns and cloud resource usage.

**Webhook Notifications**
Integrate cost alerts into your existing workflows. Cost Optix supports Slack, Microsoft Teams (Workflow and Legacy), Discord, and custom HTTP endpoints.

## Who is Cost Optix for?

- **Engineering teams** who need resource-level cost attribution to understand which services or workloads are driving spend
- **Finance teams** who need budget tracking, forecasting, and audit-ready cost reports
- **Executives** who need high-level cloud portfolio health and spend trends

## Architecture Overview

Cost Optix is a SaaS platform hosted at [costoptix.com](https://costoptix.com). Your cloud provider credentials are stored securely, and cost data is fetched on a scheduled basis using a three-tier caching strategy (Redis → local database → live provider API) to keep your dashboard fast and up to date.

For Kubernetes clusters, an optional lightweight agent can be deployed directly inside your cluster to collect real-time metrics without exposing your kubeconfig to external services.
