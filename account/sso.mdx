## Overview

Single Sign-On lets your team authenticate with Cost Optix through your existing identity provider (IdP), such as Okta, Azure Active Directory, Google Workspace, or any SAML 2.0 / OIDC-compatible provider.

SSO provides:
- Centralised access management through your IdP
- Automatic account deprovisioning when users are removed from your IdP
- Compliance-friendly audit trails
- No separate Cost Optix passwords to manage

---

## Why SSO Requires Business or Enterprise

SSO requires dedicated infrastructure to maintain isolated identity provider sessions per organization. Starter and Professional customers share infrastructure, which is incompatible with the per-organization session isolation SSO requires.

Business tier organizations run on a dedicated CloudCluster VM. Enterprise organizations deploy on their own infrastructure.

---

## Configuring SSO

To configure SSO for your organization, contact [support@costoptix.com](mailto:support@costoptix.com) with the following:

1. Your Cost Optix organization ID (found in **Settings → Organization**)
2. Your identity provider name (e.g. Okta, Azure AD, Google Workspace)
3. Whether you need SAML 2.0 or OIDC

The Cost Optix team will provide you with:
- The **SSO callback URL** to register in your IdP
- The **Entity ID** (for SAML)
- The **Audience URI**

You will then provide:
- Your IdP **metadata URL** or **metadata XML** (SAML), or your **OIDC discovery URL**
- The **client credentials** (for OIDC)

## Supported Identity Providers

| Provider | Protocol |
|---|---|
| Okta | SAML 2.0 / OIDC |
| Microsoft Azure Active Directory | SAML 2.0 / OIDC |
| Google Workspace | OIDC |
| Auth0 | SAML 2.0 / OIDC |
| OneLogin | SAML 2.0 |
| Any SAML 2.0-compliant IdP | SAML 2.0 |
| Any OIDC-compliant IdP | OIDC |

---

## User Provisioning

When a user authenticates via SSO for the first time, Cost Optix will automatically create their account within your organization if their email domain matches your registered domain.

Existing users who previously used email/password authentication can link their account to SSO on their first SSO login.

---

## Revoking SSO Access

To remove a user's access, remove them from the relevant group or application in your IdP. Their Cost Optix session will be invalidated at next login. You can also manually deactivate users from **Settings → Team Members** at any time.
